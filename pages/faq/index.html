Blitz FAQ

What is Blitz Wallet?

Blitz Wallet is a self-custodial Bitcoin-only wallet. This means you, and only you, have control of the funds in your wallet. By leveraging multiple technologies, we aim to provide a seamless payment experience allowing anyone to transact over the Bitcoin network.

What is Bitcoin?

Bitcoin is digital money that works on the internet. It allows people to send money directly to each other without needing a trusted third party. It runs on a public, secure network called a blockchain that keeps track of all transactions.

How is Blitz Wallet Different?

Blitz Wallet is designed for you, providing custody and security in a simple user interface. The contacts section of Blitz Wallet allows you to easily send and request payments to friends creating a user experience not seen by self-custodial wallets. Also, by creating a shop page directly in the wallet you can live off Bitcoin without downloading any other apps.

What payments can I send and receive?

Currently Blitz Wallet supports receiving Bitcoin, Lighting, and Liquid payments and sending Bitcoin and Liquid payments.

How does Blitz use Ecash?

Blitz wallet uses Liquid to Lighting, and Lighting to liquid swaps from Boltz until a lighting channel is open. Currently, the minimum swap amount is 1,000 satoshis or 0.00001 of a Bitcoin. In many places, this is too expensive and excludes users from being able to make transactions. So, Blitz wallet uses Ecash as pay to send and receive lighting payments under 1,000 sats.

How to Gift Bitcoin to Anyone,Even If They Don't Have a Wallet

Send Bitcoin as a gift in seconds with Blitz Gifts. Share a link, they tap to claim.

|

Imagine handing someone a $20 bill and having them say, "Thanks, but I don't have pockets." That's essentially what gifting Bitcoin has felt like, until now.

Most guides on how to gift Bitcoin assume the recipient already has a wallet set up, an exchange account verified, and at least a basic understanding of seed phrases. In other words, they assume the hard part is already done. For the vast majority of people you want to send Bitcoin to — your sister, your coworker, the barista who makes your morning coffee — none of that is true.

Blitz Gifts changes the equation entirely. You create the gift. You share a link. They tap to claim. No wallet required on their end.

Here's everything you need to know.

Why Bitcoin Makes a Better Gift Than Cash or Gift Cards

Cash loses value. Gift cards expire, carry fees, and are often forgotten in junk drawers. Bitcoin, on the other hand, is a bearer asset with a fixed supply — meaning no central bank can inflate it away, and no retailer can go bankrupt and strand your balance.

A Bitcoin gift, even a small one, is an invitation. It's a reason for someone to start learning, start paying attention, and potentially start a habit that compounds over decades. There's no better way to onboard someone to Bitcoin than to put a little in their hands first.

The Problem With Gifting Bitcoin the Traditional Way

If you've ever tried to actually send Bitcoin to someone who doesn't have it yet, you know the friction is brutal.

The traditional path looks something like this: tell them to download a Wallet, explain what a wallet address is, hope they don't get overwhelmed copying and pasting a long string of characters, and then explain why the transaction is taking 10–60 minutes to confirm or why they had to incur a fee when receving a payment.

By the time they've jumped through all those hoops, the moment is gone. The birthday dinner is over. The holiday vibe has evaporated.

What Are Blitz Gifts?

Blitz Gifts is a feature inside Blitz Wallet — a self-custodial Bitcoin Lightning wallet — that lets you package up any amount of Bitcoin and share it as a claimable link.

Think of it like a digital envelope. You put Bitcoin inside, seal it, and hand it to someone. They open it on their own terms. No coordination required. No accounts for them to create upfront.

The gift lives on the Spark Network, which means it settles in seconds and works with tiny amounts — you can send someone $0.1 or $100 with the same ease.

Critically, Blitz Wallet is self-custodial. That means you hold your own keys. The Bitcoin in a Blitz Gift is yours until the moment the recipient claims it — no middleman holding it in escrow, no platform that can freeze or lose your funds.

This also means if the Blitz Gift is never claimed, you can easily recover your funds so no Bitcoin is lost.

Step-by-Step: How to Send a Blitz Gift

Sending a Bitcoin gift with Blitz takes about 60 seconds.

Step 1 — Open Blitz Wallet If you don't have it yet, download Blitz Wallet and create your wallet. The setup takes a few minutes and you're in full control of your funds from day one.

Step 2 — Navigate to Blitz Gifts From the home screen, tap the profile icon or your contacts profile image in the top right of the screen, or on older versions the gift icon in the tabs bar.

Step 3 — Set Your Gift Amount You can either create a Bitcoin gift, which will be denominated in BTC, or USD gifts which will be denominated in USD. You can gift as little as a few hundred sats or as much as you want.

Step 4 — Add a Personal Note (Optional) Write a short message to go along with the gift. A birthday message, a "welcome to Bitcoin" note, or just your name — it shows up when the recipient opens their gift.

Step 5 — Confirm and Generate the Link Blitz creates a unique, one-time claim link for your gift. The Bitcoin is locked into that gift until it's claimed or it's expired it.

Step 6 — Share It Send the link however you normally communicate — iMessage, WhatsApp, email, even a QR code printed on a card. The recipient doesn't need Blitz installed to open the link.

How the Recipient Claims It

This is where Blitz Gifts really shines.

The recipient taps the link and lands on a clean claim page. No signup required at this stage. They can see the gift amount, your personal note, and a simple prompt to claim.

If they already have Blitz Wallet, they will already be linked direcly to the app when clicking on the claim link.

If they don't have a wallet yet, the page guides them to download Blitz Wallet — and the moment they're set up, they can claim the gift directly into their new self-custodial wallet. Their first Bitcoin experience is opening a gift from someone they know. That's a powerful first impression.

The whole experience is designed to feel like opening a birthday card, not filling out a bank form.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the recipient need to download an app to claim the gift?

No — not immediately. They can open the claim link in any browser. If they already have Blitz downloaded the Gift will automatically open the application. If they're new to Bitcoin, the page will prompt them to download Blitz Wallet so they have somewhere to receive the funds.

Does the gift expire?

Blitz Gifts have customizable claim windows. If the recipient doesn't claim within that period, the creator of the gift can then reclaim the funds inside of the gift. You'll want to remind them to claim it sooner rather than later.

Is there a fee to send a Blitz Gift?

There are currently no fees to create or claim Blitz Gifts.

What if the recipient loses the link?

Each gift link is unique and one-time use. If it's lost before claiming, you can re-view the gift link in the Blitz Wallet app to reshare with them.

Is this safe? Could someone intercept the link?

The gift link works like a bearer instrument — whoever has the link can claim it. Treat it like cash: don't post it publicly, send it directly to the intended recipient. For larger amounts, share it through a private, encrypted channel.
